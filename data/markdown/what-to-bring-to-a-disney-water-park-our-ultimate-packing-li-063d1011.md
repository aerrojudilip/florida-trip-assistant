What Are Disney’s Blizzard Beach and Typhoon Lagoon?
Before we dive (ha, dive!) into what you should bring to the Disney water parks, let's quickly recap what they are (and what there is to do). Typhoon Lagoon and Blizzard Beach are the two water parks on Walt Disney World property. Disney's Typhoon Lagoon opened in 1989, and Blizzard Beach a few years later in 1995.
Each water park has a unique theme. Disney's Typhoon Lagoon is a tropical paradise that formed after a storm, while Blizzard Beach is a melting ski resort. Normally, these water parks operate on a rotating basis, with one open while the other undergoes a seasonal refurbishment. This summer, Typhoon Lagoon and Blizzard Beach will operate simultaneously! While water park wait times are not available on the My Disney Experience app, you'll still want to use it for mobile ordering at Lottawatta Lodge (Blizzard Beach) and Leaning Palms (Typhoon Lagoon).
Both water parks offer plenty to see and do. At Typhoon Lagoon, take the plunge on Crush 'n' Gusher — a coaster-like water raft ride. Or, splash around in the massive surf pool! (Did you know the Typhoon Lagoon Surf Pool is the largest wave pool in North America?)
Headed to Blizzard Beach? Find your courage and plummet down Summit Plummet, one of the tallest and fastest free-fall body slides around! Or, unwind in relaxing Melt-Away Bay. You're on vacation, after all!
Experience all four Walt Disney World Theme Parks this summer with the 4-Day, 4-Park Magic Ticket! Valid for arrivals from May 26 to Sept. 26, 2026.
Guests visiting between May 26 and Sept. 8, 2026, can take advantage of extra Disney magic thanks to Cool Kids' Summer! Expect fun and exciting character experiences (including Goofy in his beach attire at Blizzard Beach and Stitch at Typhoon Lagoon), beach dance parties and more! Check out our Cool Kids' Summer guide to find out what's hoppening!
Additionally, if you're staying at a Disney Resort hotel, you get free admission to one water park on your arrival day from May 26 to Sept. 8, 2026. (More on this below!) Can't decide which water park to visit? Let us help you weigh the similarities and differences between Blizzard Beach and Typhoon Lagoon.
What to Bring to A Disney Water Park
The Essentials
A day spent at either Disney's Typhoon Lagoon or Disney's Blizzard Beach is nothing short of magical. Regardless of which one you're visiting (or if you decide to visit both during your stay), you're going to want to pack the essentials.
This includes:
- Bathing suit (and a change of dry clothes)
- Flip flops and/or water shoes
- Waterproof sunscreen
- Sunglasses
- Portable phone charger
- Hair ties/hair brush/detangler
- Waterproof pouch (for phone, money and other necessities)
- Goggles
- Swim shirts
- Hats
- Large zip-top bags for wet clothes
- Waterproof camera
- Deodorant
- Cooling towel/personal misting fan
Have you visited a water park only to see what another family brought and thought to yourself, "Wait, why didn't I think of that?!" Well, I've got you covered. Here are some less common (but incredibly helpful) things to bring to the water park, as well:
- Anti-chafing stick
- SPF lip balm
- Leave-in conditioner
- Foldable tote bag and/or small mesh laundry bag
- A chip clip to keep the towel on the back of your chair in place
For Families With Tadpoles
Traveling to the Disney water parks with some tadpoles in tow? In addition to the essentials listed above, you'll also want to bring:
- Wipes
- Changing pad
- Swim diapers
- US Coast Guard/UL-approved floaties
- Child-friendly sunscreen
- Hooded towels
- Waterproof AirTag
- Anti-slip water shoes
- Non-perishable snacks (pretzels, chips, crackers or trail mix)
- Bottles/sippy cups
- Formula
- Rash guards
- Diaper bag and/or backpack
When it comes to packing swim diapers, bring more than you think you need — generally one for every two hours you're at the water park — just in case of emergency. Also, keep in mind that backpacks (and all bags and coolers in general) must not be larger than 24" x 15" x 18".
If you plan on bringing your tadpole's favorite comfort toy or stuffie (especially if this is their first time away from home), buy a spare before your trip. There's nothing worse than their beloved toy getting wet at the water park — or worse, lost. Trust me …
For Families With Medical Needs
For guests who have medical needs or are traveling with someone who does, preparing for a water park visit is paramount!
For people with diabetes, this may include items such as insulin, backup pumps and/or sensors, fast-acting carbs or a small bottle of juice and adhesive medical tape. Individuals with asthma should bring a waterproof case for their inhaler. And for those with conditions like epilepsy, bring a medical ID bracelet, polarized sunglasses and/or goggles, cooling gear and a swim buddy.
While this is not an exhaustive list, it's always important to plan. During the summer, Florida temperatures can exceed 90 degrees Farheneit, which can affect your body if not prepared. Both water parks have a First Aid location that can store any medication or medical supplies that need refrigeration.
For Families With Sensory Needs
Families traveling with guests who have sensory needs will also want to plan ahead. It’s a good idea to bring waterproof fidget toys, scentless sunscreen, polarized sunglasses, gentle face-cleaning wipes and waterproof earplugs (when not in the pool).
If you need to get a break from the heat or crowds, both Disney’s Typhoon Lagoon and Disney’s Blizzard Beach offer plenty of shaded seating. We recommend getting to the water parks as soon as they open so you can reserve the best seats. Another option is to rent one of the umbrellas on site or the cabanas — Beachcomber Shacks at Disney’s Typhoon Lagoon or the Polar Patios at Disney’s Blizzard Beach.
You know your child or family member best, so pack what you know they will need to help them have a successful day at the Disney water parks.
For Families Visiting the Water Park on Travel Day
Last year, Walt Disney World introduced a complimentary water park pass to resort guests on the day of their arrival. We're hoppy to announce that this perk is back again between May 26 and Sept. 8, 2026, and will return in summer 2027 (exact dates TBD).
This water park benefit is extremely awesome and can definitely help you make the most of your time at Walt Disney World. Honestly, a day in the surf pool after traveling all morning sounds DELIGHTFUL.
That being said, you’ll want to plan ahead and focus on your essentials. Think of this as your grab-and-go-splish-splash kit:
- Travel-size sunscreen
- Mini hairbrush
- Medicine, if necessary
- Flip flops and sunglasses (ideally, wear these while traveling, so that you can just arrive at your hotel and go)
The goal is to pack light so you can start your vacation as fast as possible. Our best tip is to pack this little grab-and-go kit the night before your trip and lay it on top so it's easy to grab out of your suitcase. You will want to stop by the resort first, so you can drop your luggage off at Bell Services.
Remember, this perk is ONLY available to guests of Walt Disney World Resort hotels on the day of their arrival.
*Luggage is not permitted at either Disney’s Typhoon Lagoon or Disney’s Blizzard Beach.
What Not to Bring to the Disney Water Parks
It's just as important not to overpack for the Disney water parks. Similarly, you'll want to go over the water park policies listed on Walt Disney World's website to avoid bringing in something that's not allowed.
Per Disney's guidelines, do not bring the following into either Typhoon Lagoon or Blizzard Beach:
- Alcohol
- Selfie sticks
- Loose and/or dry ice
- Drones
- Wagons
- Folding chairs
- Coolers, backpacks or bags bigger than 24" long x 15" wide x 18" high
- Denim jeans or neoprene wetsuits (not allowed on certain rides)
For the full list of prohibited items, click here.
For your convenience, do not bring:
- Glass containers (except for baby food jars)
- Perishable food that doesn't do well in heat
- A full wallet (you can load up cash on a Disney e-gift card that you can scan from your Apple wallet; if you're staying at a Disney Resort hotel, you can use your MagicBand+ to charge purchases)
- Jewelry (leave them in the safe back in your room)
- Pool toys or inflatables
- Electronics
- Towels (both water parks provide them for free)
- Lifejackets (also complimentary)
- Makeup
The less you bring, the easier it'll be to travel back and forth to the water parks. Aside from guests who are staying at Disney's All-Star Resorts and Disney's Coronado Springs Resort, there is no direct bus service to and from Disney's Blizzard Beach and the other resorts. All other guests will have to take a bus to Disney's Animal Kingdom and then transfer. Likewise, the same applies to Disney's Typhoon Lagoon. There is no direct bus service to this water park from any of the Walt Disney World Resort hotels. Instead, guests will need to take the bus to Disney Springs, then transfer.
If you are driving a car, parking is free at the water parks. But keep in mind that during busy summer days, the parking lots can fill up quickly, and you may be directed to overflow parking. Arrive early to avoid having to park further away.
Lockers and Storage Tips
Now, once you're at the water parks, where do you put all your stuff? It depends on your personal preference and comfort level, and whether you have any valuables. If you have to bring something valuable, then getting a locker is toadally worth it for peace of mind! We will always get a locker if Tad brings his GoPro in case he wants to store it. That said, keep in mind you'll have to go to the front of the park every time you need something from the locker.
Locker rentals are available at each water park. A standard locker (12.5" x 10" x 17") costs $10 per day, while a large locker (15.5" x 13" x 17") costs $15 per day, so you have access all day long. When you purchase a locker, you will create a four-digit PIN that grants access.
If you're bringing the bare minimum, you can get away with leaving non-valuable items, such as your shoes, water bottle and sunscreen, at your chair while you go on slides or wade in the lazy river.
- For phone storage, as we mentioned previously, we recommend a waterproof case or pouch to carry it around with you (bonus points if it can fit a credit card and/or ID for adults 21+ who may want an alcoholic beverage). You may be asked to hold it in your hand (as opposed to around your neck) on certain slides.
- For money needs, we recommend using a Disney e-gift card that you can scan from your phone's digital wallet. And if you're staying at a Disney Resort hotel, you can set up a MagicBand+ to charge purchases to your room's folio.
